# DATA BREACH RESPONSE PLAYBOOK
## Emergency Response Guide for South African Organizations

---

## 🚨 IMMEDIATE RESPONSE (First 24 Hours)

### Hour 0-1: DETECTION & ASSESSMENT

**Step 1: Confirm the Breach**
- [ ] Verify breach is real (not false alarm)
- [ ] Document what happened
- [ ] Identify affected systems/data
- [ ] Determine scope (how many records/people affected)

**Step 2: Activate Response Team**
Call immediate meeting with:
- [ ] Information Officer
- [ ] IT Manager/Security Lead
- [ ] Legal Counsel
- [ ] CEO/MD
- [ ] Communications/PR (if needed)

**Step 3: Contain the Breach**
- [ ] Isolate affected systems
- [ ] Disable compromised accounts
- [ ] Change passwords/credentials
- [ ] Preserve evidence (logs, system images)
- [ ] DO NOT delete anything (evidence needed)

---

### Hour 1-4: INVESTIGATION

**Step 4: Assess Impact**
Determine:
- [ ] What personal information was accessed/stolen?
- [ ] How many data subjects affected?
- [ ] Is the data sensitive (financial, health, children's)?
- [ ] Can the breach cause harm (identity theft, fraud)?

**Step 5: Identify Root Cause**
- [ ] How did the breach occur?
- [ ] Was it external attack or internal error?
- [ ] What vulnerabilities were exploited?
- [ ] When did the breach start?

**Step 6: Document Everything**
- [ ] Timeline of events
- [ ] Actions taken
- [ ] Decisions made
- [ ] Communications sent

---

### Hour 4-24: NOTIFICATION DECISION

**Step 7: Determine if Notification Required**

POPIA Section 22 requires notification when:
- Personal information has been accessed/-acquired by unauthorized person
- AND there is a real risk of harm to data subjects

**Notification Required?**
- [ ] YES → Continue to notification steps
- [ ] NO → Document decision and continue monitoring

---

## 📢 NOTIFICATION PROCESS

### To the Information Regulator (Required)

**Timeline:** As soon as reasonably possible (aim for within 72 hours)

**Contact:**
- Website: https://inforegulator.org.za/
- Email: inforeg@inforegulator.org.za
- Phone: [check current contact details]

**Required Information:**
1. Description of breach (what happened)
2. Categories and approximate number of affected data subjects
3. Likely consequences of the breach
4. Measures taken/proposed to address breach
5. Contact details for more information

**Template Letter:** See Appendix A

---

### To Affected Data Subjects (Required)

**Timeline:** Without undue delay

**Required Information:**
1. Clear description of what happened
2. What information was involved
3. What you are doing about it
4. Steps they should take to protect themselves
5. Contact details for questions

**Template Letter:** See Appendix B

---

### To Law Enforcement (If Criminal Activity Suspected)

**Contact:**
- SAPS Cybercrime Unit
- Case number required for insurance claims

---

## 🔧 POST-BREACH ACTIONS

### Week 1: Remediation

- [ ] Fix vulnerabilities that allowed breach
- [ ] Update security measures
- [ ] Review and update policies
- [ ] Provide additional staff training
- [ ] Monitor for further suspicious activity

### Week 2-4: Review

- [ ] Conduct post-incident review
- [ ] Document lessons learned
- [ ] Update incident response plan
- [ ] Review insurance coverage
- [ ] Prepare for potential regulatory follow-up

## 📋 BREACH ASSESSMENT WORKSHEET

### Severity Assessment

| Factor | Low (1) | Medium (2) | High (3) |
|--------|---------|------------|----------|
| Data Sensitivity | Public info | Contact details | Financial/Health/ID |
| Number Affected | < 10 | 10-100 | > 100 |
| Harm Potential | None | Mild inconvenience | Financial loss/Identity theft |
| Ease of Misuse | Very difficult | Possible | Easy |

**Total Score:** _____

**Risk Level:**
- 4-6: LOW - Internal resolution, optional notification
- 7-9: MEDIUM - Consider notification, document decision
- 10-12: HIGH - Mandatory notification, regulatory involvement

## ⚠️ IMPORTANT REMINDERS

1. **Act Fast:** Delays can increase harm and regulatory penalties
2. **Document Everything:** You may need to prove you responded appropriately
3. **Be Transparent:** Honesty with affected parties builds trust
4. **Get Legal Advice:** Complex breaches need professional guidance
5. **Learn and Improve:** Use this to strengthen your security
