## SLIDE 2: What Is POPIA?

**Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013**

South Africa's data protection law that:
- Protects people's personal information
- Sets rules for how organizations handle data
- Gives people rights over their information
- Applies to ALL businesses in South Africa

**Key Point:** POPIA is not optional — compliance is legally required.

---

## SLIDE 3: Why Should You Care?

**Personal Impact:**
- You have the right to privacy too
- Your data is protected by the same law

**Professional Impact:**
- Non-compliance = fines up to R10 million
- Criminal charges possible (up to 10 years)
- Reputation damage
- Loss of customer trust

**Job Impact:**
- You play a role in protecting data
- Mistakes can have serious consequences
- Training helps you do your job safely

---

## SLIDE 4: What Is "Personal Information"?

Any information that can identify a person:

**Examples:**
- ✉️ Name and surname
- 📞 Contact details (phone, email, address)
- 🆔 ID number or passport number
- 📅 Date of birth
- 💰 Financial information
- 🏥 Health information
- 👨‍👩‍👧 Family information
- 🏢 Employment history
- 📍 Location data
- 🖼️ Photos and videos

**Remember:** If it can identify someone, it's personal information!

## SLIDE 5: Your Responsibilities

**Every Employee Must:**

1. **Handle Data Carefully**
   - Only access what you need for your job
   - Don't share passwords
   - Lock your computer when away

2. **Keep Information Confidential**
   - Don't discuss customer data in public
   - Don't share data with unauthorized people
   - Be careful on phone calls

3. **Report Problems Immediately**
   - Lost laptop? Tell IT immediately
   - Suspicious email? Report it
   - Data breach? Alert your manager

---

## SLIDE 6: The 8 Conditions for Lawful Processing

POPIA says we can only process personal information if:

1. **Accountability** — Someone is responsible (Information Officer)
2. **Processing Limitation** — We have a good reason
3. **Purpose Specification** — We tell people why we need it
4. **Further Processing Limitation** — We use it only for that purpose
5. **Information Quality** — We keep it accurate and up to date
6. **Openness** — We're transparent about what we do
7. **Security Safeguards** — We protect it properly
8. **Data Subject Participation** — People can see and correct their info

---

## SLIDE 7: What You Can and Cannot Do

✅ **YOU CAN:**
- Access data you need for your job
- Use data for approved business purposes
- Share data with authorized colleagues
- Report security concerns

❌ **YOU CANNOT:**
- Access data "just because"
- Share data with friends or family
- Take data when you leave the company
- Use customer data for personal benefit
- Snoop on colleagues or customers

---

## SLIDE 8: Password Security

**Strong Passwords:**
- Minimum 12 characters
- Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols
- Not dictionary words
- Different for each account

**Password Don'ts:**
- ❌ Don't write passwords on sticky notes
- ❌ Don't share passwords with colleagues
- ❌ Don't use "Password123"
- ❌ Don't reuse work passwords for personal accounts

**Password Managers:**
- We recommend using [Bitwarden/1Password]
- Stores passwords securely
- Generates strong passwords
- You only remember ONE master password

---

## SLIDE 9: Email and Communication Security

**Be Careful With:**

**Phishing Emails:**
- Fake emails trying to steal information
- May look like they're from banks, SARS, etc.
- Often have urgent or threatening language
- Hover over links before clicking
- When in doubt, DELETE

**Sensitive Information:**
- Never send passwords via email
- Don't email unencrypted ID documents
- Use secure file sharing for sensitive docs

**Work vs Personal:**
- Use work email for work only
- Don't forward work emails to personal accounts

---

## SLIDE 10: Physical Security

**In the Office:**
- Lock your computer (Windows+L) when away
- Lock filing cabinets with personal data
- Don't leave documents on desks overnight
- Use shredder for sensitive documents
- Don't let unauthorized people into secure areas

**Out of Office:**
- Don't discuss sensitive info in public
- Be careful on phone calls in public
- Keep work devices secure
- Report lost/stolen devices immediately

**Clean Desk Policy:**
- Clear your desk at end of day
- Lock away sensitive documents
- No passwords written down

## SLIDE 11: Recognizing a Data Breach

**What Is a Data Breach?**
When personal information is:
- Accessed by unauthorized people
- Lost or stolen
- Accidentally deleted
- Sent to wrong person

**Common Causes:**
- Lost laptop or phone
- Hacked email account
- Sending email to wrong recipient
- Paper documents left in public
- Stolen files

**Your Role:**
- Be aware of what a breach looks like
- Report anything suspicious immediately
- Better to report and be wrong than stay silent

---

## SLIDE 12: How to Report a Breach

**If You Suspect a Breach:**

1. **STOP** — Don't make it worse
2. **PRESERVE** — Don't delete anything
3. **REPORT** — Tell your manager immediately
4. **DOCUMENT** — Write down what happened

**Who to Contact:**
- Your direct manager
- Information Officer: [Name] | [Email] | [Phone]
- IT Department: [Contact]

**Remember:** Quick reporting can limit damage.

## SLIDE 13: Data Subject Rights

Under POPIA, people have rights:

1. **Right to Know** — What data we have and why
2. **Right to Access** — See their own data
3. **Right to Correct** — Fix inaccurate information
4. **Right to Delete** — Request deletion (in some cases)
5. **Right to Object** — Say no to marketing

**What This Means for You:**
- If someone asks about their data, refer them to the Information Officer
- Don't argue with requests — escalate them
- Respond professionally and promptly

---

## SLIDE 14: Direct Marketing Rules

**Electronic Marketing (Email, SMS, WhatsApp):**

✅ **We CAN market to:**
- People who gave explicit consent
- Existing customers (for similar products)

❌ **We CANNOT:**
- Send marketing without consent
- Hide the sender identity
- Make it hard to unsubscribe
- Buy email lists and spam people

**Unsubscribe Requests:**
- Must be processed within reasonable time
- Must be as easy as subscribing
- Keep records of unsubscribes

---

## SLIDE 15: Social Engineering

**What Is Social Engineering?**
Manipulating people into giving away confidential information.

**Common Tactics:**
- Pretending to be IT support
- Urgent requests from "CEO"
- Fake customer complaints
- Offering prizes or refunds

**How to Protect Yourself:**
- Verify identity before sharing information
- Be suspicious of urgent requests
- Check email addresses carefully
- When in doubt, verify through another channel

**Famous Example:**
"I'm from IT and need your password to fix something" — NEVER true!

---

## SLIDE 16: Working Remotely

**Home Office Security:**
- Secure Wi-Fi (WPA2/WPA3, strong password)
- Work in private area (not coffee shops for sensitive work)
- Lock work devices when not in use
- Don't let family use work devices

**Public Wi-Fi:**
- Avoid for sensitive work
- Use VPN if necessary
- Don't access sensitive accounts

**Personal Devices:**
- Keep work and personal separate when possible
- Use security software
- Keep devices updated

## SLIDE 18: Quiz Answers

**Scenario 1:** Email mistake
- Report to manager and Information Officer immediately
- May be breach requiring notification
- Document what was sent and to whom

**Scenario 2:** Password email
- DO NOT CLICK THE LINK
- Report as phishing
- Log in through official website if concerned

**Scenario 3:** Found USB
- DO NOT PLUG IT IN
- Give to IT department
- Could contain malware

**Scenario 4:** Data subject request
- Refer to Information Officer
- Do not handle directly
- Must be responded to within 30 days

**Scenario 5:** Stolen laptop
- Report to police (get case number)
- Report to IT and Information Officer immediately
- May be data breach
- Remote wipe if possible
