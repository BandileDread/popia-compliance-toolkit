# DATA SUBJECT ACCESS REQUEST (DSAR) WORKFLOW
## Complete Guide to Handling Data Subject Rights Requests
### POPIA Section 22-24 Compliance

---

## 1. OVERVIEW

### 1.1 What is a DSAR?

A Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) is a request from an individual (data subject) to exercise their rights under POPIA regarding their personal information.

### 1.2 Data Subject Rights Under POPIA

| Right | Section | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Right to Access** | Section 23 | Request confirmation of processing and copies of personal information |
| **Right to Correction** | Section 24 | Request correction or updating of inaccurate information |
| **Right to Deletion** | Section 24(1)(c) | Request deletion of personal information (with limitations) |
| **Right to Object** | Section 11(3) | Object to processing based on legitimate interest or direct marketing |
| **Right to Withdraw Consent** | Section 11(2) | Withdraw consent at any time |
| **Right to Complain** | Section 74 | Lodge complaint with Information Regulator |

### 1.3 Who Can Make a Request?

- The data subject themselves (with proof of identity)
- A parent/guardian (for children under 18)
- A legal representative (with authority)
- A person authorized in writing by the data subject

## 3. IDENTITY VERIFICATION

### 3.1 Why Verify?

Identity verification is REQUIRED before disclosing personal information to prevent unauthorized access and data breaches.

### 3.2 Verification Requirements by Request Type

| Requester Type | Required Verification |
|----------------|----------------------|
| **Data Subject (Adult)** | Certified copy of ID document (or passport/driver's license) |
| **Child (Under 18)** | Parent/guardian ID + child's birth certificate |
| **Legal Representative** | Authority letter + representative ID + subject ID |
| **Authorized Person** | Written authorization + both parties' IDs |
| **Deceased Estate** | Death certificate + executor appointment letter |

### 3.3 Verification Process

**Step 1: Request Documentation**
- Send verification request within 2 working days of receiving DSAR
- Provide secure upload method (encrypted email or portal)
- Set deadline for response (suggest 14 days)

**Step 2: Verify Documents**
- Check ID document authenticity
- Verify authorization letters are signed and dated
- Confirm relationship (for parents/guardians)

**Step 3: Document Verification**
- Record verification date and method
- Retain copy of verification documents (securely)
- Note any concerns or red flags

### 3.4 Insufficient Verification

If verification is insufficient:
- Request additional documentation
- Explain exactly what is needed and why
- Pause timeline until adequate verification received
- Do not disclose any information

## 4. REQUEST ASSESSMENT AND SCOPING

### 4.1 Clarify the Request

If the request is unclear:
- Contact the requester for clarification
- Ask specific questions about what information they want
- Provide examples of what information you may hold
- Document all communications

### 4.2 Define the Scope

Determine:
- **What information** is being requested?
- **Time period** covered?
- **Systems/departments** involved?
- **Format** requested (electronic/paper/inspection)?

### 4.3 Identify Information Holders

| Information Type | Likely Holder |
|------------------|---------------|
| HR/Payroll | Human Resources |
| Customer Records | Sales/CRM |
| Financial | Finance/Accounting |
| Marketing | Marketing Department |
| IT Logs | IT/Security |
| Third-Party | External processors |

---

## 5. INFORMATION SEARCH AND RETRIEVAL

### 5.1 Search Checklist

For each identified system/department:

- [ ] Search primary systems (CRM, ERP, HR systems)
- [ ] Check email archives
- [ ] Review backup systems (if necessary)
- [ ] Search shared drives and document management
- [ ] Query third-party processors (if applicable)
- [ ] Check for archived/offline records

### 5.2 Search Log

Maintain a record of:
- Systems searched
- Search terms/queries used
- Date of search
- Person conducting search
- Results found (summary only)

### 5.3 Information Found Checklist

Categorize findings:

| Category | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Subject's personal information** | Include in response (subject to exclusions) |
| **Other people's information** | Redact or exclude |
| **Legally privileged information** | Exclude (legal advice) |
| **Confidential commercial information** | Assess and potentially exclude |
| **Irrelevant information** | Exclude |

---

## 6. LEGAL REVIEW AND EXCLUSIONS

### 6.1 Grounds for Refusal or Redaction

You may refuse or redact information if:

| Ground | When Applies |
|--------|--------------|
| **Other individuals' data** | Would breach their privacy |
| **Legal privilege** | Communications with legal advisors |
| **Confidential commercial info** | Trade secrets, sensitive business data |
| **Crime prevention/detection** | Would prejudice investigations |
| **Negotiations** | Could prejudice commercial negotiations |
| **Mental health** | Would cause serious harm to requester |
| **Manifestly unfounded/excessive** | Repeated identical requests, harassment |

### 6.2 Redaction Process

When providing documents:
- Redact other individuals' names and identifying details
- Clearly mark redactions (e.g., "[REDACTED - Third party privacy]")
- Provide explanation for redactions
- Consider seeking consent from third parties where appropriate

### 6.3 Legal Review

Complex cases require legal review:
- Uncertain about exclusions
- High-risk personal information
- Potential legal claims
- Commercial sensitivity concerns

## 7. PREPARING THE RESPONSE

### 7.1 Response Format

Provide information in the requested format (if reasonable):

| Format | When Appropriate |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Electronic copy** | Email, secure download link |
| **Paper copy** | If requested and reasonable |
| **Inspection in person** | For large volumes, with supervision |
| **Verbal explanation** | Rare, only for simple requests |

### 7.2 Response Content

**Standard Response Package:**

1. **Cover Letter**
   - Reference number
   - Summary of what was found
   - Any exclusions/redactions with explanations
   - Next steps and contact information

2. **Personal Information Disclosed**
   - Copies of records
   - Clear organization/indexing
   - Redactions clearly marked

3. **Supplementary Information**
   - Description of processing purposes
   - Categories of recipients
   - Retention periods
   - Data subject rights reminder

## 8. RESPONSE TIMELINE AND EXTENSIONS

### 8.1 Standard Timeline

| Stage | Timeline |
|-------|----------|
| Acknowledge receipt | Within 2 working days |
| Verify identity | Within first 7 days |
| Complete response | Within 30 days of receipt |

### 8.2 Extension Conditions

You may extend by 30 days (total 60) if:
- Request is complex
- Multiple systems involved
- Coordination with third parties needed
- High volume of information

**Extension Notice Template:**
```
Subject: DSAR Extension Notice - [Reference]

Dear [Requester],

We are writing regarding your data subject access request [Reference] 
received on [Date].

Due to [reason: complexity/volume], we require an additional 30 days 
to complete our response. 

**Revised Timeline:**
- Original deadline: [Date]
- Extended deadline: [Date]

We apologize for any inconvenience.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

### 8.3 Refusal to Respond

If refusing to respond (manifestly unfounded/excessive):
- Notify requester within 30 days
- Provide reasons
- Inform of right to complain to Information Regulator

## 10. CORRECTION AND DELETION REQUESTS

### 10.1 Correction Requests (Section 24)

**Process:**
1. Verify the requested correction is accurate
2. Request evidence if accuracy is disputed
3. Make correction within 30 days
4. Notify requester of correction
5. Inform any third parties who received the data

**Cannot Correct?**
- If accuracy is disputed, add a note to the record
- Document both versions with source attribution

### 10.2 Deletion Requests (Right to be Forgotten)

**Deletion Required When:**
- Information is no longer necessary for original purpose
- Consent withdrawn (and no other legal basis)
- Processing was unlawful
- Legal obligation requires deletion
- Child's information in online context

**Deletion Not Required When:**
- Legal obligation requires retention
- For legal claims establishment/exercise/defense
- Public interest (research, public health)
- Exercise of right to freedom of expression

**Process:**
1. Verify deletion is appropriate
2. Delete from all systems (including backups if feasible)
3. Notify third parties who received the data
4. Confirm deletion to requester

## 11. THIRD-PARTY INVOLVEMENT

### 11.1 When Third Parties Are Involved

If third parties (operators/processors) hold the data:

- Ensure compliance with DPA terms

### 11.2 Notifying Third Parties of Corrections/Deletions

When data has been shared:
- Notify recipients of corrections within 30 days
- Notify recipients of deletions within 30 days
- Document notifications

## 14. TROUBLESHOOTING COMMON ISSUES

### 14.1 "I Can't Find All the Information"

- Document search efforts thoroughly
- Explain to requester what was searched
- Provide what was found
- Offer to continue searching if reasonable

### 14.2 "The Information Is Inaccurate"

- Verify with authoritative sources
- Correct if proven inaccurate
- Add note if disputed
- Maintain audit trail

### 14.3 "This Request Is Too Broad"

- Contact requester to narrow scope
- Provide examples of information held
- Explain search limitations
- Offer to discuss what they specifically need

### 14.4 "We No Longer Have the Data"

- Explain retention/deletion policies
- Confirm data was lawfully deleted
- Provide deletion timeline
- Suggest other sources if known

---

## 15. ESCALATION AND COMPLAINTS

### 15.1 Internal Escalation

Escalate to senior management/legal if:
- High-risk personal information involved
- Potential legal claims
- Media/public interest
- Uncertainty about response

### 15.2 Information Regulator Complaints

If requester complains to Information Regulator:
- Cooperate fully with investigation
- Provide all documentation
- Respond promptly to inquiries
- Implement any required remedial actions
