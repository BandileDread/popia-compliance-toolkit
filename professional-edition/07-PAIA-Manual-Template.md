## PART 4: GROUNDS FOR REFUSAL

### 4.1 Mandatory Grounds for Refusal

We MUST refuse access to records that:
- Are protected by legal privilege
- Contain confidential commercial information
- Would compromise safety of individuals
- Would prejudice law enforcement
- Contain research information
- Contain information supplied in confidence

### 4.2 Discretionary Grounds for Refusal

We MAY refuse access to records that:
- Are subject to privacy protections (POPIA)
- Would reveal trade secrets
- Would harm the company's commercial interests
- Contain personal information of third parties
- Are privileged internal communications

### 4.3 Public Interest Override

Even if grounds for refusal exist, we may still grant access if:
- The disclosure would reveal evidence of criminal activity
- The disclosure would reveal serious wrongdoing
- The disclosure would protect public safety
- The public interest in disclosure outweighs the harm
